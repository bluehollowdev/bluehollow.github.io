My Culture: Iranian
Facts About my Culture
Iran is known for its beautiful persian carpet weaving.Carpets woven in towns like Tabriz have specific weaving techniques. With a rich history,carpet weaving still plays a critical role in the economy of modern Iran.
Iran is home to the critically endangered Asiatic Cheetah. According to the Iranian Department of Environment, only twelve are reported to live in Iran today.
Iran is one of the worlds oldest civilizations. It was part of the Achaemenid Empire or the First Persian Empire from about 550 BC to 330 BC.
Iran is home to the highest volcano in Asia, Mount Damavand. The mountain is 18,403 feet tall, and is located in Tehran, the capital of Iran.

How my unseen culture relates to my seen culture.
My unseen culture relates to my seen cultural aspects in a few ways. Holidays represent celebration, joy and connectedness. Food often brings families together, is part of every celebration, a way we show hospitality, and brings joy. In many ways, the values in my culture are reflected in everything I do.
How my cultural background will help me in the future.
My cultural background will help me in the future because it has given me an appreciation for not only my culture but that of other countries. It has given me an appreciation for other languages, cultures and customs. If I ever decide to visit Iran to meet some of my distant relatives, or learn to speak Farsi, I will have some knowledge and experience that will help me in my adventures.
